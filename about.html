The story

Why Velumin exists

I'm 19. I'm studying BSc Psychology at De Montfort University Dubai, and I've spent the better part of this year asking one question: why hasn't AI meaningfully entered the world of psychology yet?

Not therapy apps. Not chatbots. But a real, publicly accessible platform that uses verified psychological frameworks and AI to help ordinary people understand their own minds — for free, with no clinical barrier, no jargon, and no waiting list.

"Psychology should not be a privilege. It should be as accessible as a search engine — and twice as honest."

I hold certifications in Machine Learning Specialization and Deep Learning Specialization, and I've spent considerable time understanding how AI systems think. When I combined that with what I was learning in my psychology degree — personality theory, cognitive behavioural frameworks, emotional regulation models — Velumin became the obvious thing to build.

Outside of this, I write regularly — research papers, essays, and articles on trending psychological topics. I'm also working on a book exploring Gen Z's motivational and habitual patterns, alongside a practical toolkit designed for young people navigating identity, focus, and purpose in the modern world. I play football as an amateur — and honestly, sport taught me as much about psychology as any textbook.

Velumin is my first public initiative. It won't be the last.

The mission

Psychology + AI.
For everyone. Right now.

The field of psychology has centuries of knowledge about the human mind. AI has the ability to make that knowledge personal, instant, and accessible to anyone with a phone. Velumin is the bridge between the two — built by a 19-year-old who didn't want to wait for someone else to build it.
